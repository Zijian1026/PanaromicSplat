PanoramicSplat: Scaling 3D Gaussina Splatting for Single-view Large-scale Outdoor Reconstruction

Zijian Wu1,2, Mingfeng Jiang1,
1 Zhejiang Sci-Tech University   2 University of Southern California  
Paper arXiv Video Code

Abstract

Although recent research on single-view 3D reconstruction has yielded notable progress, existing methodologies still face challenges in accurately reconstructing complex and large-scale outdoor environments. In this work, we present PanoramicSplat, a novel view synthesis model based on 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS), featuring three key innovations designed for monocular large-scale scene reconstruction. First, we introduce Panoramic Field Supervision, a self-supervised paradigm that resolves single-view ambiguities through cyclic view consistency. Second, we develop a Semantic-aware Depth Regularization mechanism that injects scene semantic and depth priors into Gaussian primitives optimization to enhance the structural understanding of the scene. Third, we introduce a Radially Weighted Photometric Loss that prioritizes reconstruction fidelity at the image centers while adaptively relaxing constraints toward peripheral regions. To support our research, we constructed a comprehensive dataset of large-scale outdoor scenes using Google Earth Studio, capturing diverse urban landscapes and occluded structures. Experimental results demonstrate that our approach significantly outperforms existing monocular methods in qualitative benchmarks.

Overview

Overall Framework. A single input image is processed by a UNet to regress per-pixel 3D Gaussian parameters, which are then splatted and rendered from multiple panoramic viewpoints. Panoramic supervision—including semantic-aware depth and radially weighted photometric losses—guides the network toward both global geometric consistency and high-fidelity local detail.
